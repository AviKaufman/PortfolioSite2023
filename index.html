About Me

Hello! I'm Avi Kaufman, a passionate technologist and researcher with a triple major in Computer Science, Physics, and Mathematics at the University of Iowa, projected to graduate in May 2025. I've embarked on an academic and professional journey defined by rigorous research, creative problem-solving, and an unquenchable thirst for innovation.

A Quantum Leap into Machine Learning

In the forefront of my current explorations is Quantum Computing, particularly its fascinating convergence with machine learning. I'm part of a cutting-edge research group where I delve into the complexities of Quantum Recurrent Neural Networks (qRNNs). My goal is ambitious: to harness the power of quantum computing to revolutionize long-term memory in neural networks. By meticulously comparing qRNNs with their classical counterparts, we're on a quest to uncover breakthroughs that could redefine cognitive computational tasks.

Bridging Theory and Practicality

My academic path has been a blend of theoretical study and hands-on experience. I've designed algorithms, untangled data structures, and immersed myself in computer organization and machine architecture. Notably, my contributions to the DUNE project within the Iowa Physics Department involved developing neural networks to decode the mysteries of neutrino interactions, a testament to my ability to synthesize complex concepts into tangible outcomes.
